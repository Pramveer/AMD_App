Description

Grazoprevir is a second generation NS3/4a protease inhibitor approved for the treatment of hepatitis C virus ( HCV) in combination with elbasvir as the fixed-dose combination product Zepatier ( FDA). Use of this medication is indicated, with our without ribavirin, for the treatment of adults with HCV genotypes 1a, 1b, or 4.

NS3/4a protease is an integral part of viral replication as it is responsible for cleaving the long polypeptide produced following translation of the viral genome. By inhibiting protease activity, grazoprevir prevents the formation of structural and nonstructural proteins required for replication and assembly (E1, E2, NS2, NS3, NS4A, NS4B, NS5A and NS5B).

Pharmacology
Indication Grazoprevir is indicated in combination with elbasvir (as the fixed dose combination product Zepatier) with or without ribavirin for treatment of chronic HCV genotypes 1a, 1b, or 4 infection in adults.
Pharmacodynamics At a concentration 40 times the therapeutic concentration, grazoprevir does not prolong QTc to any clinically relevant extent.
Mechanism of action Grazaprevir is a second generation NS3/4a protease inhibitor use to inhibit viral HCV replication. NS3/4a protease is an integral part of viral replication as it is responsible for cleaving the long polypeptide produced following translation of the viral genome. By inhibiting protease activity, grazoprevir prevents the formation of structural and nonstructural proteins required for replication and assembly (E1, E2, NS2, NS3, NS4A, NS4B, NS5A and NS5B).

Absorption Grazoprevir was found to have an AUC of 1420 ng•hr/mL, a maximum concentration (Cmax) of 165 ng/mL, and reached peak concentrations at a median Tmax of 2 hours.
Volume of distribution

1250 L
Protein binding Grazoprevir is extensively bound (98.8%) to human plasma proteins; it binds to both human serum albumin and α1-acid glycoprotein.
Metabolism

Grazoprevir is partially eliminated by oxidative metabolism, primarily by CYP3A. No circulating metabolites of either were detected in human plasma.
Route of elimination The primary route of elimination for grazoprevir is through feces with almost all (greater than 90%) of radiolabelled dose recovered in feces compared to less than 1% in urine.
Half life The geometric mean apparent terminal half-life for grazoprevir (100 mg) is approximately 31 hours in HCV-infected subjects.

Toxicity The most commonly reported adverse reactions of all intensity (greater than or equal to 5% in placebo-controlled trials) were fatigue, headache, anemia, and nausea.
